Monte Carlo Risk Simulator (GBM)OverviewThis project is a Python-based quantitative tool designed to simulate asset price paths and evaluate financial risk using Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM). It provides probabilistic insights into market behavior by generating thousands of potential outcomes based on historical volatility and drift.Key FeaturesLive Data Integration: Fetches real-time market data via the yfinance API.Geometric Brownian Motion: Implements drift-adjusted log-normal price path modeling.Risk Analytics: Automatically calculates 95% Value at Risk (VaR) and potential "Worst-Case" scenarios.Visual Analysis: Generates price path "fan charts" to visualize the widening cone of uncertainty over a 252-day trading horizon.Financial TheoryThe simulator relies on the GBM stochastic process, which assumes that stock prices follow a random walk with a consistent drift:$$dS_t = \mu S_t dt + \sigma S_t dW_t$$By using log-normal returns, the model ensures that simulated prices remain non-negative, reflecting realistic market constraints.Technical StackLanguage: Python 3.12Libraries: NumPy (Vectorized Math), Pandas (Data Handling), Matplotlib (Visualization), yfinance (Data Sourcing)
